About Macau

Macau, also spelled Macao, is a special administrative region on the southern coast of China. It is known for its blend of Portuguese and Chinese cultures, as well as its vibrant entertainment and gaming industry.

Governance: Macau operates under the "One Country, Two Systems" principle, similar to Hong Kong. It has a high degree of autonomy and maintains separate legal and economic systems from mainland China. The Chief Executive is the head of government, and the Legislative Assembly is the legislative body responsible for making laws.
