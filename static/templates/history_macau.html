History of Macau

Macau's history is rich and diverse, shaped by centuries of cultural exchange and colonial rule. It was one of the earliest European colonies in Asia, established by the Portuguese in the 16th century.

Over the years, Macau grew into a thriving trading port and a melting pot of cultures. Its strategic location on the South China Sea made it a vital hub for maritime trade between China, Europe, and other parts of Asia.

Macau's status as a Portuguese colony lasted for over 400 years until it was handed back to China in 1999, under the principle of "One Country, Two Systems." Today, Macau is a Special Administrative Region of China, known for its vibrant culture, historic landmarks, and bustling tourism industry.
